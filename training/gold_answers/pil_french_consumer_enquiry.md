The French governing-law clause does not necessarily remove English consumer protection, and it does not by itself decide which court has jurisdiction. After Brexit, jurisdiction, applicable law and judgment enforcement must be analysed separately under the contract, English common-law rules and the Hague Conventions now in force.

### Applicable law

The Rome I Regulation continues in domestic law for applicable-law questions. A consumer contract is ordinarily governed by the law of the consumer’s habitual residence where the trader pursues or directs commercial activities there and the contract falls within those activities (Regulation (EC) 593/2008, art 6). A choice of French law is permitted, but it cannot deprive the consumer of mandatory protection available under the law that would apply absent the choice (Regulation (EC) 593/2008, art 6(2)).

The evidence should therefore show whether the French website directed activity to England: UK delivery, sterling pricing, English-language targeting, UK advertising, customer support and sales history may matter. If it did, mandatory English consumer rules can remain available despite the clause. The Consumer Rights Act 2015 may govern conformity, remedies and unfair terms where within territorial scope (Consumer Rights Act 2015).

### Jurisdiction

For proceedings begun after the Brexit transition, Brussels Recast is not the ordinary starting point. An exclusive French-court clause should be tested under the Hague Choice of Court Convention 2005, but that Convention excludes consumer contracts entered primarily for personal, family or household purposes (Hague Convention on Choice of Court Agreements 2005, art 2(1)(a)).

English jurisdiction may therefore depend on the common-law gateways and forum-conveniens principles, with permission for service abroad where required. The consumer should examine the clause’s incorporation, clarity and fairness; a hidden or one-sided jurisdiction term may be challengeable. Parallel French advice is needed before assuming an English judgment will be the most efficient remedy.

### Enforcement

The Hague Judgments Convention 2019 has applied for the United Kingdom since 1 July 2025 and can assist UK-EU recognition for proceedings within its temporal and substantive scope, subject to special consumer protections and refusal grounds (Hague Convention on the Recognition and Enforcement of Foreign Judgments in Civil or Commercial Matters 2019). It does not make every judgment automatically enforceable, and earlier proceedings or excluded matters may fall back on national enforcement law.

The consumer should preserve the order, terms, checkout screens, governing/jurisdiction clause, advertising, delivery evidence and communications; send a formal complaint; consider card or platform remedies; and compare an English claim with proceedings in France. The decisive facts are directed activity, clause wording, claim date, remedy and location of assets.
