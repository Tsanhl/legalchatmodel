**Single best answer: no. The applicant does not have to prove adultery, behaviour or separation; a statement of irretrievable breakdown is conclusive under current no-fault divorce law.**

The former five-fact regime was replaced by no-fault divorce (Divorce, Dissolution and Separation Act 2020, s 1). One or both parties apply with a statement that the marriage has broken down irretrievably, and the court must take that statement as conclusive (Matrimonial Causes Act 1973, s 1, as substituted by Divorce, Dissolution and Separation Act 2020, s 1).

The one-year fact matters for a different reason: a divorce application cannot be made during the first year of marriage (Matrimonial Causes Act 1973, s 3). Once one year has elapsed, the applicant may use the no-fault process. The old adultery, behaviour and separation facts are therefore distractors, not alternative proof requirements.
