**Single best answer: B ordinarily has an equitable easement pending registration, assuming the express grant is valid and specifically enforceable.**

The express grant of a legal easement over registered land is a registrable disposition (Land Registration Act 2002, s 27(2)(d)). Until the registration requirements are met, the disposition does not operate at law (Land Registration Act 2002, s 27(1)). Equity may nevertheless recognise the specifically enforceable grant as an equitable easement.

The distractors based on a one-year registration period or the disponee’s knowledge are wrong. Section 29 establishes the priority rule for a later registered disposition for value; knowledge alone is not a free-standing exception (Land Registration Act 2002, s 29). A legal easement may sometimes override under Schedule 3, paragraph 3, but an equitable easement does not become legal merely because it is discoverable or the grantee uses the land (Land Registration Act 2002, sch 3 para 3).

B should apply to register the grant promptly. The question asks only B’s status before registration, for which an equitable easement is the best answer.
