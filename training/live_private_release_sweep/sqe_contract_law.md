**Single best answer: the contract was formed on Tuesday when A posted the acceptance, assuming post was contemplated and the offer did not require receipt. B’s Wednesday revocation was too late.**

Under the postal rule, a properly posted acceptance ordinarily takes effect on posting rather than delivery (*Adams v Lindsell* (1818) 1 B & Ald 681; *Household Fire and Carriage Accident Insurance Co Ltd v Grant* (1879) 4 Ex D 216). A therefore accepted on Tuesday even though the letter arrived on Thursday.

Revocation of an offer is effective only when communicated, not when sent (*Byrne v Van Tienhoven* (1880) 5 CPD 344). B’s email was received on Wednesday, but by then the postal acceptance had already formed the contract.

The principal alternative is that the offer required actual notice or receipt, which displaces the postal rule (*Holwell Securities Ltd v Hughes* [1974] 1 WLR 155). On that wording, B’s communicated Wednesday revocation would precede Thursday receipt. Nothing in the stated facts indicates such wording, so Tuesday is the single best answer.
