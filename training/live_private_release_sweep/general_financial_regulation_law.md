The start-up should not launch until it has completed a documented FCA perimeter analysis. “Online investment platform” can describe several regulated models, and the answer depends on the investments, customer journey, order flow, custody, advice, remuneration and whether the firm operates a marketplace or merely provides neutral technology.

### Regulated activities and authorisation

No person may carry on a regulated activity in the United Kingdom by way of business unless authorised or exempt (Financial Services and Markets Act 2000, s 19). The firm should map each function against the regulated-activities perimeter (Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (Regulated Activities) Order 2001, SI 2001/544). Potential activities include arranging deals, dealing as agent or principal, advising on investments, safeguarding and administering assets, and operating a multilateral or crowdfunding system.

Labels and contractual disclaimers do not decide the perimeter. The start-up should create a flow diagram showing who selects products, transmits orders, contracts with investors and issuers, receives money, holds assets, executes trades and earns fees. It should then seek FCA perimeter advice and specialist counsel. Possible structures include direct Part 4A permission, acting as an appointed representative within a principal’s genuine responsibility, or redesigning the service to remain outside specified activities; each has conditions and risks (Financial Services and Markets Act 2000, pt 4A).

### Financial promotions

An unauthorised person must not, in the course of business, communicate an invitation or inducement to engage in investment activity unless an authorised person approves it or an exemption applies (Financial Services and Markets Act 2000, s 21). Websites, app screens, social media, influencer material and onboarding prompts can all be communications. Every audience and promotion should be classified before publication, with evidence of approval and compliance with the applicable content rules.

### Client assets and conduct

If the platform receives or controls client money or custody assets, it must determine whether the FCA Client Assets Sourcebook applies. Using a payment provider does not necessarily remove responsibility if the platform itself receives, holds or controls funds. The design should segregate client money, reconcile records, address acknowledgement letters and resolution planning where CASS applies.

Authorised firms must also identify the relevant conduct sourcebooks, product-governance rules, appropriateness or suitability obligations, complaints and Financial Ombudsman exposure. Retail business may engage the Consumer Duty, requiring good-faith conduct, avoidance of foreseeable harm and support for retail customers under the FCA’s current rules (FCA Handbook, PRIN 2A). Cybersecurity, operational resilience, UK GDPR, AML and sanctions controls should be built into the model.

The practical next step is a regulated-activities matrix and pre-application gap analysis covering controllers, capital, governance, senior managers, systems, wind-down, outsourcing and client assets. The firm should verify the live FCA Handbook and permissions immediately before applying; authorisation is activity-specific and a pending application does not permit launch.
