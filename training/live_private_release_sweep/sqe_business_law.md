**Single best answer: the director’s duty to declare an interest in a proposed transaction under section 177 is most directly engaged.**

A director interested directly or indirectly in a proposed transaction or arrangement with the company must declare the nature and extent of that interest to the other directors before the company enters the transaction (Companies Act 2006, s 177). Because the interest is undisclosed and the transaction is only proposed, section 177 matches the facts precisely.

The general duty to avoid conflicts is relevant background, but it does not apply to a conflict arising in relation to a transaction or arrangement with the company in the same way (Companies Act 2006, s 175(3)). Section 182 concerns declaration of an interest in an existing transaction, so it is not the best answer at the pre-transaction stage (Companies Act 2006, s 182).

The director should disclose fully before the board commits the company and comply with the constitution and any approval requirements.
