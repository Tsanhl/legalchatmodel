# International Trade Law

## Answer Method
- Start with the trade regime: WTO/GATT, GATS, trade remedies, security exception, regional trade agreement, or domestic implementation.
- Problems should separate prima facie breach, exception, justification, procedure, remedy and compliance.
- Essays need a thesis on liberalisation, sovereignty, development, security, environmental protection, or institutional legitimacy.

## Topic Map
- GATT principles, MFN, national treatment, quantitative restrictions, exceptions, subsidies, safeguards, trade remedies, WTO dispute settlement, security exceptions, development and fragmentation.

## Material-Led Emphasis
- Trade answers should identify measure, product/service, complainant/respondent, obligation and comparator before policy.
- GATT/GATS problems need breach and exception separated: Article I/III/XI or market-access rule first, then Article XX/XXI or specific defence.
- Trade remedies need injury, dumping/subsidy/surge, causation, investigation procedure and proportionality of measure.
- WTO dispute-settlement and appellate-body status are current-law sensitive and should be verified when procedural remedy is central.

## For Each Topic Include
- Identify the measure, product/service, comparator and obligation.
- Test exceptions separately from breach.
- End with likely dispute outcome and remedy/compliance consequence.

## Case Brief Bank
- US - Gasoline: Facts: environmental regulation affected imported gasoline. Held: Article XX can justify measures only if chapeau is satisfied. Reasoning: prevent arbitrary or unjustifiable discrimination. Answer use: Article XX structure.
- EC - Asbestos: Facts: ban on asbestos products. Held: health protection could justify trade restriction. Reasoning: product likeness and risk matter. Answer use: national treatment/health.
- US - Shrimp: Facts: turtle-protection import restrictions. Held: environmental measure can fit Article XX but must respect chapeau. Reasoning: unilateralism and discrimination controlled. Answer use: Article XX and process methods.
- Russia - Traffic in Transit: Facts: security exception invoked. Held: Article XXI has justiciable good-faith limits. Reasoning: self-judging language is not unlimited. Answer use: security exception.
- China - Rare Earths: Facts: export restrictions. Held: conservation exception unavailable where treaty commitments/conditions not met. Reasoning: textual discipline. Answer use: export controls/resources.

## Feedback Rules
- Do not treat exceptions as free policy discretion; apply the textual test and chapeau.
- Identify comparator and likeness before national-treatment conclusions.
- For security, separate emergency, essential security interest, nexus and good faith.
- In fairness essays, distinguish formal non-discrimination from development capacity and bargaining inequality.

## Topic-Specific Marking Rubric
- Top-band answers state obligation, breach, exception, chapeau/proportionality-like discipline, remedy and compliance.
- Strong essays link trade liberalisation to sovereignty, environment, development and institutional legitimacy with examples.
- Weaker answers use pro/anti-globalisation commentary without treaty route.

## Strong First-Class Accuracy Pass
- For Article XXI/security, separate self-judging language, good faith, emergency, essential security interest and nexus.
- For fairness/development essays, distinguish formal equality from material inequality and bargaining power.
- Current WTO reform/dispute-settlement status must be verified before being stated as settled.

## Avoid
- Avoid treating WTO exceptions as free-standing policy preferences.
- Avoid assuming trade liberalisation and fairness are identical.
