# Financial Regulation Law

## Answer Method
- Start with the regulatory objective and institution: FCA conduct regulation, PRA prudential regulation, systemic risk, market abuse, consumer protection, enforcement or redress.
- Problems should separate authorisation, rule breach, systems/controls, individual responsibility, enforcement route, redress and sanctions.
- Essays need a thesis on risk prevention, market integrity, consumer protection, proportionality, innovation or regulatory failure.

## Topic Map
- Authorisation, conduct of business, prudential supervision, systems and controls, senior managers, market abuse, financial promotions, enforcement, redress, systemic risk and innovation.

## Material-Led Emphasis
- Financial-regulation answers should identify regulated activity, person, product, client category and regulator before discussing policy.
- Enforcement problems need rule source, breach, systems/controls failure, individual responsibility, consumer loss, sanction and redress route.
- Market abuse answers should separate insider dealing, unlawful disclosure, manipulation, disclosure duties and civil/criminal consequences.
- Innovation/crypto/fintech answers are current-law sensitive and should verify regulatory perimeter and guidance.

## For Each Topic Include
- Identify the regulated person, activity, rule source and regulator.
- Separate private compensation from public enforcement.
- End with likely regulatory consequence and practical compliance step.

## Case Brief Bank
- FCA v Arch Financial Products: Facts: investment manager conflicts and unsuitable investments. Held: breaches of regulatory duties justified enforcement and compensation. Reasoning: client protection and systems failures. Answer use: conduct enforcement.
- Plevin v Paragon Personal Finance: Facts: undisclosed PPI commission. Held: unfair relationship under consumer credit legislation. Reasoning: disclosure and fairness can matter outside FCA enforcement. Answer use: redress/private law overlap.
- Financial Services Authority v Anderson: Facts: unauthorised collective investment scheme. Held: regulatory perimeter and consumer protection justified intervention. Reasoning: authorisation is central. Answer use: unauthorised business.
- FCA v Macris: Facts: identification in enforcement notices. Held: procedural rights may arise where an individual is identified. Reasoning: fairness in regulatory enforcement. Answer use: notices/third-party rights.
- R (Bluefin Insurance Services) v FCA: Facts: challenge to regulator findings. Held: regulatory discretion and tribunal routes matter. Reasoning: public-law challenge is limited where statutory process exists. Answer use: enforcement challenge.

## Feedback Rules
- Name the FCA/PRA Handbook area or statutory route where possible; do not rely on general prudence.
- Separate public enforcement, private law compensation, FOS complaints and FSCS protection.
- Do not infer rule breach from financial loss alone; identify suitability, disclosure, authorisation, systems or market-integrity duty.
- For senior managers, link personal responsibility to function, knowledge, reasonable steps and governance evidence.

## Topic-Specific Marking Rubric
- Top-band answers identify activity, perimeter, rule source, client category, breach, enforcement/redress route and sanction.
- Strong essays balance consumer protection, market integrity, innovation and systemic risk with institutional competence.
- Weaker answers merge prudential and conduct regulation or omit the precise regulator and remedy.

## Strong First-Class Accuracy Pass
- Current FCA/PRA rules and guidance are update-sensitive; verify live rules before relying on them.
- Keep prudential risk, conduct risk and market-integrity risk distinct.
- Do not infer breach merely from loss; identify the rule, duty or standard.

## Full OSCOLA Authority Bank
- Regulatory perimeter/enforcement: Financial Services and Markets Act 2000, ss 19, 21, 55L, 66, 138D and 206; Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (Regulated Activities) Order 2001, SI 2001/544.
- Consumer credit/redress: Consumer Credit Act 1974, ss 140A-140C; *Plevin v Paragon Personal Finance Ltd* [2014] UKSC 61, [2014] 1 WLR 4222.
- Enforcement/fairness: *FCA v Arch Financial Products LLP* [2021] EWCA Civ 1183; *Financial Conduct Authority v Macris* [2017] UKSC 19, [2017] 1 WLR 1459.
- Market abuse/SMCR: UK Market Abuse Regulation, arts 7, 14, 15, 17 and 19; Senior Managers and Certification Regime materials under FSMA 2000 and FCA/PRA rules.
- FSMA 2000 (as amended); Financial Services and Markets Act 2023 reforms as engaged.
- Regulatory objectives / accountability: check current FCA/PRA statutory objectives text.
- Market abuse / listing: UK MAR (assimilated) / FSMA Part 8 as engaged; *FCA v Da Vinci Invest Ltd* [2015] EWHC 2401 (Ch) (illustrative — verify before pinpoints).
- Consumer duty / SMCR: treat Handbook rules as regulatory soft-law unless incorporated; do not cite Handbook paragraphs as if statutes.

## Current-Law Update Checkpoints
- FCA Consumer Duty is live for open products and services from 31 July 2023 and closed products and services from 31 July 2024. Use it for regulated-firm conduct/outcomes but still identify the exact Handbook/statutory route.
- Cryptoasset financial promotions have been in the FCA remit since 8 October 2023, and the FCA's 2026 material indicates a new wider cryptoasset regulatory regime with pre-application meetings from 11 May 2026 and meetings from July 2026. Verify whether the facts concern promotions, AML registration, or future authorisation.

## Avoid
- Avoid generic "regulator will act" conclusions without route and sanction.
- Avoid mixing banking private-law duties with public regulatory enforcement unless both are raised.
