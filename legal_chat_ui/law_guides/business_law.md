# Business Law and Company Law

## Answer Method
- Identify business form and issue: company, partnership, agency, directors’ duties, shareholder remedies, insolvency, or corporate governance.
- Problems need actor-by-actor advice: company, directors, shareholders, creditors, partners, office-holder.
- Essays need a thesis on separate personality, accountability, creditor protection, shareholder primacy, or governance.
- Keep company law separate from contract/tort unless routes overlap.

## Topic Map
- Corporate personality, veil piercing, authority, directors’ duties, derivative claims, unfair prejudice.
- Partnership formation, authority, liability, dissolution.
- Insolvency: wrongful trading, misfeasance, preferences, transactions at undervalue, administration/liquidation.
- Business and human rights / supply-chain due diligence where asked.

## Material-Led Emphasis
- Business-practice answers should start with the vehicle because authority, liability, tax, filings, and remedies change with partnership/LLP/company status.
- Company-law problems need board authority, shareholder approval, directors' duties, conflicts, filings, and remedy/enforcement route in separate sections.
- Shareholder-remedy answers should separate company loss from personal shareholder prejudice and explain derivative/unfair-prejudice routes.
- Insolvency/security answers need priority, validity/perfection, voidability, director exposure, and practical recovery prospects.

## For Each Topic Include
- Directors: identify duty, conflict/benefit, authorisation/ratification, causation/remedy.
- Shareholder remedies: standing, unfairly prejudicial conduct, relief, derivative permission.
- Insolvency: trigger date, office-holder claim, connected party, relief/contribution.

## Case Brief Bank
- Salomon: Facts: incorporated business debt. Held: company is separate legal person. Reasoning: incorporation creates distinct entity. Answer use: corporate personality.
- Prest v Petrodel: Facts: matrimonial assets and companies. Held: veil piercing narrow; property rules often enough. Reasoning: concealment/evasion distinction. Answer use: veil.
- Regal (Hastings): Facts: directors profited from opportunity. Held: account of profits. Reasoning: strict no-conflict/no-profit. Answer use: directors’ duties.
- West Mercia: Facts: insolvent company and creditor interests. Held: creditor-duty shift near insolvency. Reasoning: residual claim changes. Answer use: insolvency duties.
- O’Neill v Phillips: Facts: unfair prejudice expectations. Held: equitable constraints on strict rights in small company. Reasoning: legitimate expectations must be grounded. Answer use: s 994.
- Re Produce Marketing Consortium: Facts: insolvent trading. Held: directors liable for wrongful trading contribution. Reasoning: knowledge and minimising loss matter. Answer use: wrongful trading.

## Feedback Rules
- Separate company claim, shareholder claim, and creditor/office-holder claim.
- Do not overuse veil piercing where agency, trust, tort, or statutory route is better.
- Insolvency answers improve when trigger dates and director-specific fault are explicit.
- Director-conflict answers should not say majority shareholding solves the issue. Check board authority, articles, quorum, declaration of interest, shareholder approval, ratification limits, and remedy.
- Related-party asset transactions require separate treatment of directors' duties, substantial property transaction approval, valuation evidence, and the enforcement route.
- Insolvency-adjacent facts need a date-sensitive analysis: when the creditor-interest duty arose, what each director knew or ought to have known, and what steps were taken to minimise loss.

## Topic-Specific Marking Rubric
- Top-band company answers identify who the client is and who owns the claim: company, shareholder, creditor, office-holder, partner, or third party.
- Top-band directors' duties answers separate ss.171-177 duties, authorisation/ratification, causation, account of profit/indemnity/rescission, and shareholder remedies.
- Top-band insolvency answers separate wrongful trading, misfeasance, transactions at undervalue, preferences, distributions, security validity, priority and practical recovery.
- Weaker answers usually treat directors as one block, assume majority control cures breach, or mention veil piercing without explaining why a narrower route does not work.

## Strong First-Class Accuracy Pass
- Start with the legal vehicle and client identity. Company, LLP, partnership, shareholder, director, creditor and office-holder routes create different duties and remedies.
- Director-conflict answers must check board authority, articles, quorum, declaration of interest, shareholder approval, ratification limits and remedy. Majority shareholding is not a cure by itself.
- Related-party transactions need directors' duties, substantial property transaction approval, valuation evidence, connected-person status and enforcement route separated.
- Shareholder remedies require company loss versus personal prejudice, derivative permission, unfair prejudice relief, reflective-loss limits and likely order.
- Insolvency facts need trigger date, creditor-interest duty, director-specific knowledge, wrongful trading/misfeasance/voidable transaction route, security priority and realistic recovery.

## Full OSCOLA Authority Bank
- Corporate personality and directors: Companies Act 2006, ss 171-177, 239 and 260-264; *Salomon v A Salomon & Co Ltd* [1897] AC 22 (HL); *Regal (Hastings) Ltd v Gulliver* [1942] 1 All ER 378 (HL); *BTI 2014 LLC v Sequana SA* [2022] UKSC 25, [2024] AC 211.
- Shareholder remedies: Companies Act 2006, ss 994-996; *Foss v Harbottle* (1843) 2 Hare 461, 67 ER 189; *O'Neill v Phillips* [1999] 1 WLR 1092 (HL).
- Insolvency: Insolvency Act 1986, ss 212-214, 238, 239 and 423; Company Directors Disqualification Act 1986, ss 6 and 8.
- Partnership: Partnership Act 1890, ss 1, 5, 9, 24, 29 and 30; Limited Liability Partnerships Act 2000, s 1.

## Avoid
- Avoid moral blame without duty and remedy.
- Avoid treating all directors collectively if facts differentiate them.

## Anonymized Output Architecture
- A conflict or connected-transaction problem should identify who owns each claim, test board authority and voting rules, analyse each director separately, then move through approval or ratification, shareholder routes and remedy. Majority control does not itself cleanse a conflict.
- An insolvency answer should build a dated solvency timeline, distinguish each director's knowledge, separate creditor-interest governance from office-holder causes of action, and rank realistic recoveries after priority and evidential constraints.
