# Consumer Law

## Answer Method
- Classify the transaction first: goods, digital content, services, mixed contract, subscription, unfair practice, product safety, or consumer credit.
- Confirm that the parties are a trader and consumer before applying consumer-only controls.
- Problems should proceed through information/representation, implied statutory rights, breach, statutory remedy sequence, unfair terms, enforcement and any parallel private-law claim.
- Current-law answers must check commencement: the Consumer Rights Act 2015 remains central, while the Digital Markets, Competition and Consumers Act 2024 reforms unfair-practice enforcement, subscriptions and consumer ADR in stages.

## For Each Topic Include
- Scope and definitions; subject matter; statutory standard; whether contracting out is prohibited.
- The correct remedy ladder and its time/notice requirements.
- Transparency, prominence and fairness of terms; private remedy versus regulator enforcement.
- Any overlap with misrepresentation, contract damages, product liability or data protection without double recovery.

## Case Brief Bank
- Consumer Rights Act 2015, pt 1: statutory rights and remedies for goods, digital content and services; Part 2 controls unfair consumer terms and notices.
- Digital Markets, Competition and Consumers Act 2024, pt 4: unfair commercial practices, subscription contracts and consumer dispute/ADR reforms, subject to commencement and transitional rules.
- ParkingEye Ltd v Beavis: a consumer charge is assessed under the penalty rule and the statutory fairness/transparency framework; legitimate interest does not displace the consumer-law analysis.
- Office of Fair Trading v Abbey National plc: fairness review depends on the statutory framework and the nature/prominence of the term; do not turn the decision into a general immunity for price terms.

## Strong First-Class Accuracy Pass
- Do not import Sale of Goods Act business rules into a consumer contract without checking the CRA scheme.
- Separate a trader's breach from the statutory remedial consequence: repeat performance, price reduction, repair/replacement, rejection and damages are not interchangeable.
- For subscriptions or unfair practices under the 2024 Act, verify the provision's actual commencement date before advising.

## Avoid
- Avoid treating every unfair outcome as an unfair term; identify the exact statutory route.
- Avoid assuming regulator powers automatically create a private damages claim.
