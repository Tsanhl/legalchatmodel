# Privacy, Media and Defamation Law

## Answer Method
- Identify the publication/processing actor, claimant, information or statement, audience, platform role and requested remedy.
- Separate defamation, misuse of private information/breach of confidence, data protection, harassment, contempt and platform regulation.
- Map competing Articles 8 and 10 rights expressly; neither has automatic priority.

## For Each Topic Include
- Defamation: meaning, reference, publication, serious harm, cause of action, defence, intermediary rule, limitation and remedy.
- Privacy: reasonable expectation of privacy, Article 8/10 balancing, public-interest case, proportionality and injunction/damages.
- Data: controller/processor, personal/special-category data, lawful basis, transparency, security, rights, transfer, regulatory/private remedy.
- Online platforms: distinguish statutory safety duties and regulator enforcement from the publisher's private-law liability.

## Case Brief Bank
- Defamation Act 2013: serious-harm threshold; truth, honest opinion and public-interest defences; website-operator and secondary-publisher provisions; remedies.
- Lachaux v Independent Print Ltd: serious harm is a fact-sensitive statutory threshold proved by the publication's actual impact/inference, not merely the tendency of words.
- Campbell v MGN Ltd: misuse of private information asks first for a reasonable expectation of privacy and then balances Articles 8 and 10.
- Bloomberg LP v ZXC: ordinarily a person under criminal investigation before charge has a reasonable expectation of privacy, subject to the fact-sensitive balancing exercise.
- Data Protection Act 2018 and UK GDPR: operate alongside privacy/media claims, including the journalism/special-purposes regime and data-subject remedies.
- Data (Use and Access) Act 2025: amends the UK data-protection framework in stages; verify commencement for lawful bases, purpose limitation, automated decision-making and other reforms.
- Online Safety Act 2023: platform safety duties and Ofcom enforcement must not be confused with a general private right to damages for all harmful content.

## Strong First-Class Accuracy Pass
- Apply the exact serious-harm evidence and each pleaded defence; do not stop at defamatory meaning.
- In privacy cases, perform the structured Article 8/10 balance using the actual information, claimant, publisher, contribution to debate and intrusion/publication method.
- State whether a cited statutory reform is in force on the relevant date.

## Avoid
- Avoid treating falsity as a complete privacy answer or truth as a complete data-protection answer.
- Avoid assuming platform regulation replaces publisher/intermediary liability analysis.

## Full OSCOLA Authority Bank
- Misuse of private information / confidence: *Campbell v MGN Ltd* [2004] UKHL 22, [2004] 2 AC 457; *Douglas v Hello! Ltd* [2007] UKHL 21, [2008] 1 AC 1; *PJS v News Group Newspapers Ltd* [2016] UKSC 26, [2016] AC 1081.
- Defamation: Defamation Act 2013; *Lachaux v Independent Print Ltd* [2019] UKSC 27, [2020] AC 612.
- Data overlap: UK GDPR / DPA 2018; *Lloyd v Google LLC* [2021] UKSC 50.
- Open justice / anonymity: *Scott v Scott* [1913] AC 417; *PJS* [2016] UKSC 26.
- Harassment: Protection from Harassment Act 1997; *Majrowski v Guy's and St Thomas's NHS Trust* [2006] UKSC 34.
- Data + journalism exemption: DPA 2018 Sch 2 Part 5 — verify before advising.

## Current-Law Update Checkpoints
- Separate privacy, defamation, data protection, and harassment routes.
