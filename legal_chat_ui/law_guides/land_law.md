# Land Law

## Answer Method
- Identify the land-law subfield first: registration, estates/interests, lease/licence, co-ownership, easements, covenants, mortgages, priority, or estoppel.
- Problems need transaction chronology and priority analysis before remedies; the first practical question is usually whether the land is registered.
- Essays need a thesis about certainty, fairness, registration, numerus clausus, or family-home protection.
- Always distinguish legal and equitable interests.

## Topic Map
- Registered/unregistered land; overriding interests; overreaching; priority.
- Co-ownership and family home: resulting trust, common-intention constructive trust, proprietary estoppel, TOLATA.
- Easements and covenants; leases/licences; mortgages and undue influence; adverse possession.

## Material-Led Emphasis
- Problem answers should sequence: identify the proprietary right, check creation/formality, check registration/protection, then priority and remedy.
- Start by identifying who is being advised and what they want: binding a purchaser, resisting possession, obtaining a declaration, enforcing a covenant/easement, or securing relief.
- Proprietary estoppel answers must keep assurance, reliance, detriment, unconscionability, and remedy proportionality separate.
- Adverse possession answers need registered/unregistered distinction, factual possession, intention to possess, time, notices/objections, and statutory exceptions.
- Lease/easement/covenant answers must avoid label-based conclusions; apply the validity test and then transmission/protection.

## For Each Topic Include
- Registration: identify interest, method of protection, priority rule, overriding/overreaching possibility.
- Co-ownership: legal title -> beneficial shares -> acquisition -> quantification -> priority/remedy.
- Easement/covenant: validity -> benefit/burden transmission -> registration -> remedies/modification.
- Short/business leases: contractual term and expiry -> whether still unexpired at completion -> if expired/about to expire, check Landlord and Tenant Act 1954 Part II security for business occupation unless validly contracted out -> consequence for vacant possession.
- Options/estate contracts: creation -> need for notice -> effect of buyer's official search with priority -> whether urgent notice/interim relief is needed before completion.

## Case Brief Bank
- Williams & Glyn’s Bank v Boland: Facts: spouse in actual occupation. Held: beneficial interest could override. Reasoning: occupation gave notice-independent priority. Answer use: actual occupation.
- Stack v Dowden: Facts: domestic co-ownership shares. Held: equity follows law unless contrary common intention. Reasoning: whole course of dealing matters. Answer use: joint names.
- Jones v Kernott: Facts: separated co-owners and changing shares. Held: court may infer/impute intention on shares. Reasoning: fairness operates through intention framework. Answer use: quantification.
- Guest v Guest: Facts: farm succession assurance. Held: remedy must satisfy equity proportionately. Reasoning: expectation and detriment both matter. Answer use: proprietary estoppel remedy.
- Street v Mountford: Facts: occupancy agreement labelled licence. Held: exclusive possession for term at rent indicated lease. Reasoning: substance over label. Answer use: lease/licence.
- Re Ellenborough Park: Facts: claimed park-use easement. Held: easement valid if four requirements met. Reasoning: accommodation and capable grant matter. Answer use: easement test.

## Feedback Rules
- Do not dismiss domestic contributions in one sentence; link them to acquisition, quantification, reliance, or detriment.
- Problem answers should follow the basic route: identify the legal issues, state the relevant legal principles, then apply those principles to the facts. Do not rehearse the problem facts as a substitute for analysis.
- Each factual paragraph should be doing land-law work: creation, formality, registration/protection, priority, overreaching, overriding status, or remedy. Remove facts that do not affect one of those steps.
- Keep overreaching and overriding interests separate.
- Remedies must be explicit: declaration, TOLATA order, injunction, damages, or estoppel relief.
- Course-bound answers should respect the taught topic boundaries unless the prompt expressly expands them. Do not import housing-allocation, homelessness, or unrelated public-law reform material into a land-law private-rights question.
- Essay questions often include a quotation or proposition for a reason; use it as the analytical target, not as a decorative opening line.
- Registered-land problem answers should not just name the LRA 2002. They must apply s.27 registrable-disposition consequences, s.29 priority, notices/restrictions, and Schedule 3 para 2 exceptions where the facts raise them.
- Deal separately with each asserted right. An easement, lease, option to purchase, beneficial interest, and consent/restriction arrangement may travel through different creation and priority routes.
- If actual occupation is relevant, identify occupation, discoverability/inquiry, consent, overreaching, and exceptions in a disciplined sequence rather than treating occupation as an automatic trump.
- If facts say a lease was granted for a fixed term some years ago, calculate whether it has expired or is about to expire. For business occupation, do not stop at expiry; check statutory continuation/security under the Landlord and Tenant Act 1954 unless the facts show valid contracting out.
- Purchase-money contributions should trigger resulting trust analysis as well as common-intention constructive trust evidence. Keep acquisition, quantification, priority against a lender, and distribution of sale proceeds separate.
- Where a lender's registered charge is subject to an overriding beneficial interest, explain the practical priority: the charge may bite only on the registered proprietor's beneficial share unless the interest is overreached on a later sale.
- For driveway facts, separate right of way from parking. Parking may be capable of being an easement, but only if it does not deprive the servient owner of reasonable use. Visibility of use is not the same thing as registration or priority.
- For options and estate contracts close to completion, discuss whether a buyer's official search with priority blocks late notice protection and whether interim relief is needed.
- Overreaching clears beneficial interests under a trust only if appointment/payment to two trustees is valid. It does not overreach leases, easement-type claims, or options.
- Use party-by-party or right-by-right headings where the scenario asks advice to multiple parties. The structure should mirror the question's requested advice rather than a generic lecture order.

## Topic-Specific Marking Rubric
- Top-band registered-land answers start with registered/unregistered status and then move right-by-right through creation, registration/protection, priority, and remedy.
- Top-band co-ownership answers separate acquisition from quantification and then from TOLATA/remedy; they do not treat broad fairness as a free-standing title rule.
- Top-band estoppel answers run assurance, reliance, detriment, unconscionability, and proportionality of relief separately.
- Weaker answers usually apply unregistered rules to registered land, overlook separate options/easements, ignore overreaching, or state actual occupation without exceptions.
- Strong first-class registered-priority answers catch date traps: expired business leases/Landlord and Tenant Act 1954, official-search priority before late notices, lender priority against only the chargor's beneficial share, and valid two-trustee overreaching.

## Strong First-Class Accuracy Pass
- Start with land status, then classify each asserted right separately. Do not decide priority before creation/formality and protection route.
- Registered priority needs LRA 2002 s.27/s.29 logic, notices/restrictions, overriding interests and Schedule 3 exceptions. Actual occupation and overreaching are different mechanisms.
- Lease questions must calculate expiry. Business occupation requires Landlord and Tenant Act 1954 security checks before assuming vacant possession.
- Co-ownership needs acquisition, quantification, lender priority, sale proceeds and overreaching separated. Purchase-money contributions may trigger resulting trust as well as common intention analysis.
- Easements/options require creation, deed/registration, actual use, notices, official search with priority and interim relief where completion is imminent.

## Full OSCOLA Authority Bank
- Formalities and priority: Law of Property Act 1925, ss 2, 52-53; Land Registration Act 2002, ss 27, 29 and Sch 3 para 2; Landlord and Tenant Act 1954, Pt II.
- Overreaching/occupation: *Williams & Glyn's Bank Ltd v Boland* [1981] AC 487 (HL); *City of London Building Society v Flegg* [1988] AC 54 (HL); *Thompson v Foy* [2009] EWHC 1076 (Ch).
- Co-ownership: *Stack v Dowden* [2007] UKHL 17, [2007] 2 AC 432; *Jones v Kernott* [2011] UKSC 53, [2012] 1 AC 776; *Williams v Hensman* (1861) 1 John & H 546, 70 ER 862; Trusts of Land and Appointment of Trustees Act 1996, ss 14-15; Law of Property Act 1925, s 36.
- Leases/easements/estoppel: *Street v Mountford* [1985] AC 809 (HL); *Re Ellenborough Park* [1956] Ch 131 (CA); *Wheeldon v Burrows* (1879) 12 Ch D 31 (CA); *Guest v Guest* [2022] UKSC 27, [2023] AC 111; Prescription Act 1833, s 2; Law of Property Act 1925, s 62; Land Registration Act 2002, Sch 3 para 3.
- Covenants/mortgages: *Tulk v Moxhay* (1848) 2 Ph 774, 41 ER 1143; *Royal Bank of Scotland plc v Etridge (No 2)* [2001] UKHL 44, [2002] 2 AC 773.

## Avoid
- Avoid broad fairness without property concepts.
- Avoid priority conclusions before identifying the interest and protection route.

## Anonymized Quality Upgrade
- A severance answer should begin with a direct qualified thesis, use the statutory route first, distinguish the recognised methods and use contrasting cases to explain why the facts fall on one side of the line.
- High-level analysis adds an identified reform position, academic debate, explicit ranking of the safest route on the facts and branches for missing matters such as actual receipt of a notice.

## Anonymized Output Architecture
- A registered-priority answer should state land status and chronology before analysing each asserted right through creation, formality, protection and priority. Actual occupation and overreaching require separate tests and separate consequences.
- A co-ownership or estoppel answer should identify the claimant's objective and legal title, separate acquisition from quantification, then address third-party priority, TOLATA or estoppel relief and why the selected remedy is proportionate.
