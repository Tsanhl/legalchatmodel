# Maritime Law

## Answer Method
- Start with the maritime regime: carriage of goods, charterparty, bill of lading, salvage, collision, limitation, marine insurance, or jurisdiction/arbitration.
- Cargo problems should separate contract document, applicable rules, carrier duty, exceptions, limitation, causation and damages.
- Salvage/collision problems need regime, threshold, reward/liability, limitation and insurance consequences.

## Topic Map
- Bills of lading, charterparties, Hague/Hague-Visby type rules, seaworthiness, care of cargo, exceptions, limitation, salvage, collision, general average and marine insurance overlap.

## Material-Led Emphasis
- Maritime answers should identify document and role first: shipper, carrier, consignee, charterer, owner, salvor, insurer or cargo interest.
- Carriage problems require contract document, incorporated rules, seaworthiness/care obligation, exception, limitation, causation and damages.
- Charterparty problems should separate hire/freight, off-hire, laytime/demurrage, safe port, deviation, frustration and termination.
- Salvage/collision answers need threshold, causation, fault apportionment, limitation and insurance consequences.

## For Each Topic Include
- Identify the contract/document and applicable regime first.
- Separate breach, causation, exceptions, limitation and remedy.
- End with likely recovery and practical claim route.

## Case Brief Bank
- The Muncaster Castle: Facts: defective vessel and due diligence. Held: carrier liable despite delegation to contractors. Reasoning: due diligence is non-delegable. Answer use: seaworthiness.
- The Rafaela S: Facts: straight bill of lading. Held: could be a bill of lading for Hague-Visby purposes. Reasoning: document function matters. Answer use: documentary route.
- The Starsin: Facts: bill identified carrier issue. Held: contract construction determines carrier identity. Reasoning: bill wording and signature box matter. Answer use: who is liable.
- The Eurasian Dream: Facts: fire and unseaworthiness. Held: causation and due diligence central. Reasoning: seaworthiness assessed with evidence. Answer use: cargo damage.
- The Tojo Maru: Facts: salvage reward and salved fund. Held: salvage reward subject to maritime principles and limits. Reasoning: reward encourages rescue but has boundaries. Answer use: salvage.

## Feedback Rules
- Do not cite Hague-Visby without explaining incorporation and applicability.
- Keep cargo damage, delay, misdelivery and documentary title separate.
- In charterparty answers, state whether the claim is under bill, charter, tort, bailment or insurance.
- For salvage, explain danger, voluntariness, success and reward factors.

## Topic-Specific Marking Rubric
- Top-band answers identify maritime instrument, governing rules, party status, breach/exception, limitation, causation and recovery route.
- Strong answers separate proprietary/documentary rights from contractual damages.
- Weaker answers use generic contract law and ignore bills, charters or limitation.

## Strong First-Class Accuracy Pass
- Do not assume every shipment is governed by the same carriage regime; identify incorporation and route.
- Keep cargo damage, delay, misdelivery and documentary rights separate.
- For salvage, distinguish reward, special compensation/environmental issues, and salved-fund limits.

## Avoid
- Avoid generic contract analysis before checking the maritime instrument.
- Avoid citing maritime acronyms without explaining the route they control.
