# Trusts Law

## Answer Method
- Start with the trust/fiduciary relationship and the property affected.
- Problems should separate creation, constitution, breach, tracing, defences, and remedies.
- Essays need a thesis on certainty, conscience, loyalty, proprietary remedies, or commercial flexibility.
- Distinguish personal from proprietary claims.

## Topic Map
- Three certainties, constitution, formalities, secret trusts, resulting/constructive trusts.
- Fiduciary duties: no conflict, no profit, self-dealing, secret commissions, authorisation.
- Breach of trust, knowing receipt, dishonest assistance, tracing, mixed funds, remedies.
- Charitable trusts and purpose trusts.
- Variation of trusts: Saunders v Vautier termination/variation by adult capacitated absolutely entitled beneficiaries, Variation of Trusts Act 1958 approval for minors/unborn/incapacitated or certain contingent interests, and Benjamin orders for missing-beneficiary administration.

## Material-Led Emphasis
- Tutorial/problem answers should start by classifying the trust or equitable claim before discussing remedies.
- Creation problems need separate certainties, formalities, constitution, and consequence of failure; do not merge them into a fairness discussion.
- Breach/remedy questions need chronology: trustee/fiduciary status, duty, breach, causation/loss or gain, defences, then personal/proprietary remedy.
- Constructive/resulting trust answers should identify the trigger: presumed intention, common intention, unconscionability, fiduciary breach, or receipt of property.
- Variation answers must classify the route before balancing policy: beneficiary consent under Saunders v Vautier, statutory court approval under the Variation of Trusts Act 1958, or a Benjamin order. Each route has different beneficiaries, powers and consequences.

## For Each Topic Include
- Creation: intention, subject matter, objects, formalities, constitution.
- Fiduciary breach: fiduciary status, duty, breach, consent/authorisation, remedy.
- Tracing: original property, substitute asset, mixing rule, priority, insolvency consequence.
- Variation: identify adult/capacitated beneficiaries, absolute entitlement, agreement, minor/unborn/incapacitated or contingent interests, benefit requirement, and whether missing-beneficiary assumptions preserve or extinguish rights.

## Case Brief Bank
- Knight v Knight: Facts: trust language dispute. Held: three certainties required. Reasoning: enforceability depends on clear intention/property/objects. Answer use: creation.
- Milroy v Lord: Facts: incomplete transfer. Held: equity will not perfect an imperfect gift. Reasoning: correct transfer method matters. Answer use: constitution.
- Re Rose: Facts: transferor did everything required. Held: equity may treat transfer as effective. Reasoning: unconscionability once transferor has done all. Answer use: exceptions to Milroy.
- Armitage v Nurse: Facts: trustee exemption clause. Held: core obligation of honesty cannot be excluded. Reasoning: trust requires irreducible core. Answer use: trustee liability clauses.
- Boardman v Phipps: Facts: fiduciary profited from information. Held: account of profit despite good faith. Reasoning: prophylactic loyalty rule. Answer use: no-profit duty.
- FHR European Ventures: Facts: secret commission. Held: bribe/commission held on constructive trust. Reasoning: proprietary response deters disloyalty. Answer use: remedies and insolvency.
- Saunders v Vautier: Facts: beneficiary sought trust property despite postponement. Held: adult capacitated absolutely entitled beneficiaries can terminate the trust. Reasoning: beneficial ownership can override settlor-imposed timing where all relevant beneficiaries agree. Answer use: termination/variation.
- Re Weston's Settlements / Goulding v James: Facts: court approval of trust variations affecting protected beneficiaries. Held: court approval turns on benefit, broadly understood. Reasoning: the court consents for those unable to consent but does not simply enforce settlor preference. Answer use: Variation of Trusts Act 1958 benefit analysis.

## Full OSCOLA Authority Bank
- Nature and scope of fiduciary loyalty: *Bristol and West Building Society v Mothew* [1998] Ch 1 (CA); *Bray v Ford* [1896] AC 44 (HL); *Aberdeen Railway Co v Blaikie Brothers* (1854) 1 Macq 461 (HL).
- No-conflict and no-profit rules: *Keech v Sandford* (1726) Sel Cas Ch 61, 25 ER 223; *Boardman v Phipps* [1967] 2 AC 46 (HL); *Regal (Hastings) Ltd v Gulliver* [1967] 2 AC 134 (HL).
- Honesty, authorisation and remedies: *Armitage v Nurse* [1998] Ch 241 (CA); *Murad v Al-Saraj* [2005] EWCA Civ 959, [2005] WTLR 1573; *FHR European Ventures LLP v Cedar Capital Partners LLC* [2014] UKSC 45, [2015] AC 250.
- Trust creation and constitution, only where directly relevant: *Knight v Knight* (1840) 3 Beav 148, 49 ER 58; *Milroy v Lord* (1862) 4 De GF & J 264, 45 ER 1185; *Re Rose* [1952] Ch 499 (CA); *Pennington v Waine* [2002] EWCA Civ 227, [2002] 1 WLR 2075.
- Beneficiary consent and variation, only where directly relevant: *Saunders v Vautier* (1841) 4 Beav 115, 49 ER 282; Variation of Trusts Act 1958, s 1; *Re Weston's Settlements* [1969] 1 Ch 223 (CA).

## Fiduciary-Loyalty Essay Accuracy Lock
- A constituted trustee does not ordinarily balance a continuing fiduciary duty to the settlor against a duty to beneficiaries. The quotation concerns conflicts between the fiduciary's duty to the principal/beneficiaries, personal interest, and duties owed to another person.
- Distinguish the fiduciary duty of loyalty from trustees' non-fiduciary duties of care, skill, accounting and administration. Do not call every trustee obligation fiduciary.
- Strictness is prophylactic: liability under the no-conflict/no-profit rules does not depend on fraud, bad faith, loss to the trust or proof that the opportunity would otherwise have been obtained.
- Critical evaluation must address informed consent or authorisation, the need for a real sensible possibility of conflict, equitable allowance, the choice between personal and proprietary remedies, and whether remedial flexibility qualifies rather than abolishes strict liability.
- *Armitage v Nurse* is a Court of Appeal authority reported at [1998] Ch 241; the irreducible-core formulation is associated with Millett LJ. Do not attribute it to Lord Halsbury, Lord Lindley or the House of Lords.
- *Boardman v Phipps* is reported at [1967] 2 AC 46. Boardman was a fiduciary, not the trustee whose shares were administered by the trust.
- *FHR European Ventures LLP v Cedar Capital Partners LLC* is [2014] UKSC 45. Do not substitute Pimco, call it a 2015 Court of Appeal decision, or present secret commissions as merely personal relief.
- Creation, constitution and variation cases such as *Knight v Knight*, *Re Rose*, *Saunders v Vautier* and *Re Weston's Settlements* are not evidence that fiduciary loyalty itself is flexible. Omit them from a focused loyalty essay unless a precise comparison is necessary.

## Feedback Rules
- Do not let tracing analysis swallow the prior question whether there was a breach.
- Apply the law, do not just state it. Trusts problem answers lose force if they recite certainties, fiduciary duties or variation routes without identifying which fact triggers or defeats each step.
- Remove material that is not directly relevant to the analysis. A sympathetic purpose, family context, or attractive tax result does not by itself create a trust power or equitable remedy.
- Separate equitable compensation, account of profits, rescission, lien, and constructive trust.
- Explain why proprietary relief matters in insolvency.
- Purpose-trust questions should start with the beneficiary principle, then ask whether a recognised anomalous exception applies; do not expand the exceptions merely because the purpose is sympathetic.
- Testamentary drafting/problem answers should state suggested wording only after identifying the defect it fixes, such as uncertainty, perpetuity, beneficiary principle, communication, or lack of constitution.
- Secret-trust answers must keep communication, acceptance, timing, and half-secret/fully-secret classification separate.

## Topic-Specific Marking Rubric
- Top-band creation answers run intention, subject matter, objects, formalities and constitution separately, then state what happens if the trust fails.
- Top-band purpose-trust answers distinguish animal/monument/private-mass style exceptions from ordinary private purpose trusts, and address capriciousness, enforceability and perpetuity.
- Top-band breach/tracing answers proceed chronologically: property base, fiduciary/trustee status, breach, substitute asset, mixed-fund rule, priority, defences and remedy.
- Weaker answers usually convert every fairness concern into a constructive trust, start tracing before breach, or state remedy labels without explaining personal/proprietary consequences.

## Strong First-Class Accuracy Pass
- Classify the equitable route before remedy: express trust, resulting trust, constructive trust, proprietary estoppel, fiduciary breach, knowing receipt, dishonest assistance or tracing.
- Creation answers must keep intention, subject matter, objects, formalities and constitution separate. If the trust fails, state where beneficial title goes.
- Secret-trust answers need fully secret/half-secret classification, communication, acceptance, timing, fraud rationale and Wills Act interaction.
- Tracing answers require original property, substitute asset, mixed fund, lowest intermediate balance or backwards tracing issue, volunteer/value priority and insolvency consequence.
- Remedies must state personal/proprietary effect: equitable compensation, account of profits, rescission, lien, constructive trust and subrogation do different work.

## Avoid
- Avoid calling every trustee duty fiduciary; separate loyalty from care/skill.
- Avoid remedy labels without consequence.

## Anonymized Quality Upgrade
- A testamentary-disposition problem should open with the core issues, quote only the operative words supplied in the problem, distinguish mandatory from precatory language and apply the certainties and termination rules to those words.
- For each disposition, state the consequence of failure and recipient of the beneficial interest, address workability or capriciousness where relevant, rank validity and give practical trustee steps.

## Anonymized Output Architecture
- For dispositions involving purposes or secrecy, classify each transfer first, then test certainties and formalities, the beneficiary principle and exceptions, communication and acceptance, and the destination of property if the arrangement fails.
- For breach and tracing, establish status, duty, breach and proprietary base before following value. Keep personal defendants and remedies separate from proprietary claims, apply mixing and defence rules asset by asset, and rank recovery by insolvency consequence.
