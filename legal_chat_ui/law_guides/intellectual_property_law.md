# Intellectual Property Law

## Answer Method
- Identify the right first: copyright, patent, trade mark, passing off, design, database right, confidence, or AI/data issue.
- Problems need subsistence/validity, ownership, infringement, defences/exceptions, remedies.
- Essays need a thesis on incentives, access, competition, personality, creativity, or technological change.
- Keep UK/EU/US positions separate.

## Topic Map
- Copyright subsistence, originality, authorship/ownership, restricted acts, fair dealing, communication to public.
- Patents: patentability, novelty, inventive step, sufficiency, infringement.
- Trade marks/passing off: registrability, confusion, reputation, dilution, due cause.
- AI and copyright: training data, authorship, outputs, text/data mining, platform liability.

## Material-Led Emphasis
- IP problems should start by selecting the right; do not use copyright, trade mark, passing off, and patent language interchangeably.
- Copyright answers need work category, originality, ownership, restricted act, substantial part, defence, and remedy.
- Trade mark/expression questions need use in trade, sign/goods link, origin/reputation functions, confusion or unfair advantage, due cause, and speech/competition balance.
- AI questions must separate training inputs, generated outputs, authorship/inventorship, platform liability, and exceptions.

## For Each Topic Include
- Copyright: work type -> originality -> ownership -> restricted act -> substantial part -> defence/remedy.
- Trade mark: valid registration -> use in course of trade -> sign/goods similarity -> confusion/reputation -> defence.
- Patent: valid patent -> claim construction -> infringement -> validity attack.

## Case Brief Bank
- Infopaq: Facts: newspaper text extraction. Held: originality protects author’s intellectual creation. Reasoning: even short extracts can be protected. Answer use: originality/substantial part.
- SAS Institute: Facts: software functionality and language. Held: functionality not protected as expression. Reasoning: idea/expression boundary. Answer use: software copyright.
- Designers Guild: Facts: fabric design copying. Held: substantial part is qualitative. Reasoning: protect expression, not ideas. Answer use: infringement.
- Arsenal v Reed: Facts: unofficial merchandise. Held: trade mark use can infringe where origin function affected. Reasoning: mark functions matter. Answer use: trade marks.
- Windsurfing / Pozzoli: Facts: inventive step method. Held: structured obviousness test. Reasoning: identify inventive concept and skilled person. Answer use: patents.
- Thaler: Facts: AI named as inventor. Held: inventor must be natural person under current law. Reasoning: statutory language controls. Answer use: AI inventorship.

## Feedback Rules
- Do not conflate subsistence/validity with infringement.
- State whether a point is UK, EU, or US law.
- For AI, identify the exact issue: input training, model output, authorship, liability, or exception.

## Strong First-Class Accuracy Pass
- Identify the right and jurisdiction first. Copyright, patents, trade marks, passing off, designs, database right and confidence each have different protected subject matter and remedies.
- Copyright answers need work category, originality, authorship/ownership, restricted act, substantial part, defence/exception and remedy. Do not discuss fair dealing after concluding remedy.
- Trade mark and passing-off answers should separate registered right validity, use in trade, sign/goods similarity, origin/reputation functions, confusion/unfair advantage/dilution, due cause and speech/competition arguments.
- Patent answers must construe the claim before infringement or validity. Keep novelty, inventive step, sufficiency, excluded subject matter and remedies separate.
- AI/data questions require input training, generated output, authorship/inventorship, platform liability and text/data mining or other exceptions to be separated and current-law checked.

## Current-Law Update Checkpoints
- Separate subsistence, infringement, defences/exceptions, and remedies.
- AI-training answers must verify the statutory TDM / research exception text in force on the facts' date — do not invent a US fair-use rule for UK copyright.

## Avoid
- Avoid broad “AI copyright” discussion without right and act.
- Avoid defences after remedies; analyse before remedy.

- Trade marks: Trade Marks Act 1994; *Interflora Inc v Marks & Spencer plc* [2014] EWCA Civ 1403; *Sky plc v SkyKick UK Ltd* [2021] EWCA Civ 1121 (and later Supreme Court treatment — verify before advising).
- Patents: Patents Act 1977; *Actavis UK Ltd v Eli Lilly & Co* [2017] UKSC 48, [2017] RPC 21.
- Passing off: *Reckitt & Colman Products Ltd v Borden Inc* [1990] 1 WLR 491 (HL); *Starbucks (HK) Ltd v British Sky Broadcasting Group plc* [2015] UKSC 31.
