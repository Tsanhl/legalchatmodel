# Election Law

## Answer Method
- Start with election type, regulated actor, spending/donation/reporting rule, offence, civil/regulatory consequence, petition route or public-law challenge.
- Problems should separate campaign finance, online campaigning, donation legality, spending returns, candidate/agent responsibility, criminal/regulatory sanctions and election-petition consequences.
- End with whether the breach affects result validity, creates sanction risk, or only requires correction/reporting.

## Topic Map
- Candidate spending, party spending, donations, third-party campaigning, digital adverts, spending returns, offences, Electoral Commission enforcement, election petitions and judicial review.

## Material-Led Emphasis
- Election answers should identify election type, actor status and regulated period before applying spending, donation or petition rules.
- Campaign finance breaches, criminal offences, regulator sanctions and election-petition invalidity are separate consequences.
- Digital campaigning needs imprint, spending, donation/source and data-use analysis where facts raise them.
- Judicial review is usually secondary to the statutory election route unless the complaint concerns public administration outside a petition remedy.

## For Each Topic Include
- Identify actor and regulated period.
- Separate financial reporting breach from vote-validity challenge.
- State evidential and causation hurdles for setting aside an election.

## Case Brief Bank
- Representation of the People Act 1983, s 106: prohibits specified false statements of fact about a candidate's personal character or conduct made or published for the purpose of affecting the candidate's return, subject to the statutory defence.
- Elections Act 2022, pt 6: requires promoter/publisher information on regulated electronic campaign material within the statutory scope; check current Electoral Commission guidance.
- Morgan v Simpson: Facts: local election irregularities. Held: elections may be invalid if irregularities affect the result or are so serious that election was not conducted substantially according to law. Reasoning: distinguish trivial irregularity from result-affecting illegality. Answer use: election petitions.
- R v Cripps, ex p Muldoon: Facts: election-petition procedure and electoral irregularity. Held: statutory election remedies must be followed. Reasoning: election law uses specialised routes. Answer use: procedure and exclusivity.
- Woolas election petition: Facts: false statements about candidate. Held: election was void for illegal practice. Reasoning: statutory protection against false personal statements. Answer use: campaign speech limits.
- R (Good Law Project) v Electoral Commission: Facts: regulator decision challenged. Held: public-law challenge depends on legal error in regulatory approach. Reasoning: court does not remake political merits. Answer use: Electoral Commission/JR.
- Bowman v United Kingdom: Facts: campaign spending limit interfered with political expression. Held: Article 10 was engaged. Reasoning: electoral integrity must be balanced with expression. Answer use: campaign finance and speech.

## Feedback Rules
- Do not assume every breach voids an election; ask whether petition thresholds and result effect are met.
- Identify candidate, agent, party, third-party campaigner or donor status before liability.
- Treat current Electoral Commission guidance as update-sensitive and verify thresholds where exact figures matter.
- Separate political criticism from legally regulated false statements, spending and donations.

## Topic-Specific Marking Rubric
- Top-band answers map actor, period, rule, breach, evidence, regulator/offence consequence, petition consequence and remedy.
- Strong essays balance electoral integrity, free expression, equality of arms and enforceability.
- Weaker answers give political commentary or ignore petition procedure and causation.

## Strong First-Class Accuracy Pass
- Current Electoral Commission guidance and statutory thresholds are update-sensitive.
- Do not assume every campaign breach voids an election.
- Keep criminal, civil/regulatory and petition consequences separate.

## Avoid
- Avoid political commentary without legal route.
- Avoid treating online campaigning as unregulated without checking current rules.
