# Insurance Law

## Answer Method
- Start with policy type, insured risk, disclosure/presentation duty, misrepresentation/non-disclosure, warranty/condition, causation, claim handling and remedy.
- Problems should separate pre-contract disclosure, policy construction, breach of term, fraudulent claim, indemnity and regulatory/consumer status.
- End with whether insurer may avoid, reduce, refuse, pay, or seek recovery.

## Topic Map
- Fair presentation, consumer insurance, material circumstance, inducement, warranties, conditions precedent, basis clauses, fraudulent claims, subrogation, indemnity, aggregation and remedies.

## Material-Led Emphasis
- Insurance answers should identify consumer/non-consumer status, policy wording, risk, claim event and remedy before general contract principles.
- Non-consumer fair presentation requires material circumstance, insured knowledge, reasonable search, clear presentation, inducement and proportionate remedy.
- Consumer insurance focuses on reasonable care and the insurer's response to qualifying misrepresentation.
- Claims-stage issues should separate coverage, breach of warranty/condition, fraudulent claim, late payment and subrogation.

## For Each Topic Include
- Identify consumer/non-consumer status and applicable statutory route.
- Separate materiality, knowledge, inducement and remedy.
- Apply policy wording before general fairness.

## Case Brief Bank
- Pan Atlantic Insurance v Pine Top Insurance: Facts: non-disclosure and inducement. Held: materiality and inducement both matter. Reasoning: insurer must show it was induced. Answer use: pre-2015 background/fair presentation.
- Versloot Dredging v HDI Gerling: Facts: fraudulent device used in valid claim. Held: collateral lie did not forfeit otherwise valid claim. Reasoning: proportionality and fraud policy. Answer use: fraudulent claims.
- The Star Sea: Facts: marine insurance disclosure and claims. Held: duty of utmost good faith had limits after litigation began. Reasoning: context of insurance duties. Answer use: good faith background.
- Impact Funding Solutions v AIG Europe: Facts: solicitor professional-indemnity exclusion. Held: policy wording controlled coverage. Reasoning: construction before fairness. Answer use: exclusion/coverage.
- Sprung v Royal Insurance: Facts: delayed payment of insurance claim. Held: old law gave limited damages for late payment. Reasoning: later statutory reform important. Answer use: late payment reform context.

## Feedback Rules
- Do not say avoidance automatically follows non-disclosure; identify statutory remedy and what insurer would have done.
- Separate coverage construction from breach of policy term.
- For warranties and conditions precedent, identify suspension, causation and remedy consequences.
- If fraudulent claim is raised, distinguish fraudulent claim, fraudulent device and exaggeration.

## Topic-Specific Marking Rubric
- Top-band answers state policy route, status, materiality, knowledge, inducement, wording, remedy and claims consequence.
- Strong answers distinguish Insurance Act 2015 and Consumer Insurance Act 2012 routes.
- Weaker answers treat insurance as ordinary misrepresentation/contract without statutory consequences.

## Strong First-Class Accuracy Pass
- Current statutory provisions and policy wording control the answer.
- Do not treat every non-disclosure as avoidance; remedy may depend on deliberate/reckless/careless conduct and what insurer would have done.
- Keep coverage construction and claims-handling conduct separate.

## Avoid
- Avoid assuming insurance is ordinary contract law only.
- Avoid remedy conclusions without materiality, inducement and statutory consequence.
