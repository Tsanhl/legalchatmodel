# Criminal Procedure

## Answer Method
- Identify the stage first: investigation, charge, first appearance, allocation, plea, disclosure, trial, sentence or appeal.
- State the court, governing rule/statute, burden/standard, time limit and remedy for non-compliance.
- Use the current Criminal Procedure Rules and Criminal Practice Directions; procedural rules change frequently and must be checked live for current numbering and text.

## For Each Topic Include
- Jurisdiction and venue; defendant status and representation; prosecution/defence duty.
- Applicable procedural test; evidence required; judicial discretion; fairness/prejudice.
- Immediate order, possible adjournment/exclusion/stay/costs consequence, and appeal route.

## Case Brief Bank
- Criminal Procedure Rules: overriding objective and active case management apply across the criminal courts; cite the current Part and rule only after official verification.
- Police and Criminal Evidence Act 1984: core investigation, interview and evidence-exclusion framework.
- Criminal Procedure and Investigations Act 1996: prosecution disclosure and defence-statement framework.
- R v Horseferry Road Magistrates' Court, ex p Bennett: proceedings may be stayed where abuse of process would offend the court's integrity.

## Strong First-Class Accuracy Pass
- Separate admissibility, weight, disclosure failure, abuse of process and unsafe conviction; each has a different test and remedy.
- For fitness to plead, special measures, vulnerable witnesses or hearsay, identify both the statutory test and procedural consequence.
- Verify current rule numbers and practice directions at the official Justice procedure-rules site before giving filing or deadline advice.

## Avoid
- Avoid discussing substantive offence liability when the question asks about trial procedure.
- Avoid guessing a time limit or current rule number.
