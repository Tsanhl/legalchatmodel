# Employment Law

## Answer Method
- Start by identifying the claimant’s status: employee, worker, applicant, former employee, or self-employed contractor.
- Problems need separate routes for status, statutory right, employer defence, causation, time limit, and remedy.
- Essays need a thesis on the balance between labour protection, business flexibility, managerial prerogative, and access to enforcement.
- Keep employment restrictive covenants separate from land covenants: the issue is restraint of trade, legitimate business interest, reasonableness, and severance.

## Topic Map
- Worker and employee status; personal service, control, mutuality, substitution, and contractual reality.
- Unfair dismissal: potentially fair reason, investigation, procedure, range of reasonable responses, redundancy selection, consultation, and alternatives.
- Equality and discrimination: direct, indirect, harassment, victimisation, equal pay, reasonable adjustments, and justification.
- Whistleblowing, maternity/parental rights, flexible working, working time, restrictive covenants, garden leave, and post-termination restraints.

## Material-Led Emphasis
- Problem answers should start with status and limitation/time limits because many employment rights depend on gateway eligibility.
- Redundancy/unfair-dismissal answers need reason, process, selection, consultation, alternatives, range of reasonable responses, and remedy adjustment.
- Maternity/pregnancy facts require separate automatic unfair dismissal, discrimination, and redundancy-priority routes where applicable.
- Restrictive-covenant answers should separate non-compete, non-solicitation, non-dealing, confidentiality, garden leave, severance, and injunction discretion.

## For Each Topic Include
- Status: identify the statutory route first, then apply personal service, control, mutuality, substitution, integration, and reality over written terms.
- Unfair dismissal: separate eligibility, reason, procedure, substantive fairness, contributory fault, Polkey reduction, reinstatement/re-engagement, and compensation.
- Discrimination/equal pay: classify the claim, identify comparator or PCP, burden of proof, justification, employer liability, and remedies.
- Restrictive covenants: identify the protected interest, construe the clause, test reasonableness at formation, consider severance, and state the likely injunction/damages outcome.

## Case Brief Bank
- Ready Mixed Concrete: Facts: driver engaged under contract with mixed employment indicators. Held: employee status requires wage/work, control, and consistency with employment. Reasoning: multi-factor test. Answer use: employee status baseline.
- Autoclenz: Facts: car valeters’ contracts used substitution/self-employment wording. Held: tribunal could look beyond written terms. Reasoning: inequality of bargaining power and practical reality matter. Answer use: sham/reality in status.
- Uber v Aslam: Facts: platform drivers claimed worker rights. Held: drivers were workers during app-on periods. Reasoning: statutory purpose and control outweighed contract labels. Answer use: worker status and platform control.
- British Home Stores v Burchell: Facts: dismissal after alleged misconduct. Held: employer needs genuine belief on reasonable grounds after reasonable investigation. Reasoning: fairness assessed by employer reasonableness, not court substitution. Answer use: misconduct dismissal.
- Iceland Frozen Foods: Facts: unfair dismissal challenge. Held: tribunal asks whether dismissal fell within range of reasonable responses. Reasoning: managerial discretion has boundaries. Answer use: unfair dismissal standard.
- Polkey: Facts: dismissal with procedural defects. Held: compensation may reduce if fair process would still have led to dismissal. Reasoning: causation affects remedy, not liability. Answer use: remedy adjustment.
- Bilka-Kaufhaus: Facts: part-time workers disadvantaged in pension access. Held: indirect discrimination can be justified if objectively necessary and proportionate. Reasoning: business aim must be legitimate and means appropriate. Answer use: justification structure.
- Tillman v Egon Zehnder: Facts: senior employee challenged non-compete wording. Held: impermissible words could be severed. Reasoning: severance allowed if no major rewriting and remaining restraint is supported. Answer use: restrictive covenants and severance.

## Health-and-Safety Dismissal Accuracy Lock
- If an employee is dismissed for leaving, proposing to leave, or refusing to return to a workplace in circumstances of danger which the employee reasonably believed to be serious and imminent and could not reasonably avert, begin with Employment Rights Act 1996, s 100(1)(d). Appropriate protective steps are addressed by s 100(1)(e); raising harmful or potentially harmful circumstances can engage s 100(1)(c).
- The issue is the employee's reasonable belief and the statutory conditions, not whether the tribunal later decides that the workplace was objectively unsafe. Analyse the evidence, what the employee knew, available advice, whether the danger persisted and whether the response was appropriate.
- Section 100 is an automatically unfair reason. The ordinary potentially-fair-reason/range-of-reasonable-responses route under s 98 is secondary, and *Burchell* cannot displace the automatic statutory protection.
- Section 108(3) disapplies the ordinary qualifying period for the listed automatic-unfair-dismissal grounds. As at 14 July 2026, the ordinary two-year qualifying period remains in force until the Employment Rights Act 2025 reduction to six months is commenced on 1 January 2027; this does not alter existing day-one automatic protections.
- Dismissal is defined by Employment Rights Act 1996, s 95, not s 94(2). The employer's general health-and-safety duty is in Health and Safety at Work etc Act 1974, s 2(1), not s 5.
- Do not invent facts about pay, control, the contents of the contract, risk assessments or workplace conditions. If the question calls the claimant an employee, treat status briefly and conditionally rather than turning the answer into a status survey.
- Reinstatement and re-engagement are discretionary tribunal remedies; do not promise reinstatement, full pay or any fixed award. Address basic and compensatory awards, mitigation, contribution/Polkey only where legally relevant, the statutory cap/current limits where verified, ACAS early conciliation and the applicable limitation period.

## Feedback Rules
- Do not say “unfair” generally: name the statutory claim and prove each element.
- Avoid merging employee status, worker status, and self-employment; the consequences differ.
- In problem answers, give practical remedy advice rather than ending at liability.
- Restrictive-covenant answers improve when land-law covenant language is excluded.

## Strong First-Class Accuracy Pass
- Start with status and gateway: employee, worker, applicant, former employee, qualifying period, limitation/time limit and ACAS early conciliation can decide the claim before merits.
- Unfair dismissal needs reason, reasonable investigation, procedural fairness, range of reasonable responses, Polkey, contributory fault and remedy. Do not collapse liability and compensation.
- Discrimination needs the exact route: direct, indirect, harassment, victimisation, reasonable adjustments, equal pay or pregnancy/maternity. Then identify comparator or PCP, burden, justification and remedy.
- Redundancy and pregnancy/maternity facts require special priority and automatic unfair dismissal/discrimination routes where applicable; do not treat them as ordinary redundancy only.
- Restrictive covenants need protected interest, clause construction, reasonableness at formation, severance, garden leave overlap and injunction discretion.

## Current-Law Update Checkpoints
- Employment Rights Act 2025 (c 36) received Royal Assent on 18 December 2025 and is being commenced in phases. Verify which measures are in force before treating new rights as operative.
- Government timetable material published in 2026 identifies measures taking effect at Royal Assent, 18 February 2026, 1 April 2026, 6 April 2026 and 7 April 2026, with further measures in August and October 2026. Route any answer through commencement and transitional provisions before final advice.
- As at 14 July 2026, the reduction of the ordinary unfair-dismissal qualifying period from two years to six months and removal of the compensatory-award cap are scheduled for 1 January 2027. Existing automatic-unfair-dismissal routes remain without the ordinary qualifying period.
- The extension of most Employment Tribunal limitation periods from three to six months is scheduled for 1 October 2026. Before that commencement date, apply the existing claim-specific period and ACAS early-conciliation adjustment unless the question specifies a later dismissal/claim date.

## Avoid
- Avoid treating contract labels as decisive where statutory employment protection is at stake.
- Avoid discussing every employment right if the prompt points to one statutory route.
- Avoid vague discrimination analysis without comparator, PCP, burden, justification, and remedy.
