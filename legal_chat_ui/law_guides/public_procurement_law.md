# Public Procurement Law

## Answer Method
- Start with procurement regime, contracting authority, contract type, threshold, procedure, award criteria, transparency/equal-treatment issue, standstill/challenge timing and remedy.
- Problems should separate pre-award errors, award evaluation, exclusion/selection, conflicts, manifest error, automatic suspension, damages and set-aside/remedy.
- Essays need a thesis on transparency, competition, discretion, value for money, flexibility or over-formalism.

## Topic Map
- Scope, thresholds, procedures, selection, award criteria, abnormally low tenders, conflicts, transparency, equal treatment, standstill, automatic suspension, remedies and reform.

## Material-Led Emphasis
- Procurement answers should identify the applicable regime and commencement position before applying principles.
- Challenges need stage discipline: pre-tender, selection, evaluation, award, standstill, post-contract and damages each have different remedies.
- Equal treatment and transparency require more than disappointment with scoring; identify manifest error, undisclosed criterion, unequal information, conflict or failure to give reasons.
- Remedies are timing-sensitive: suspension and set-aside are strongest before contract; damages/ineffectiveness depend on statutory thresholds after contract.

## For Each Topic Include
- Identify the applicable regime and stage.
- Separate scoring disagreement from legal error.
- End with challenge strength, remedy and commercial consequence.

## Case Brief Bank
- Letting International v Newham LBC: Facts: procurement evaluation dispute. Held: transparency and equal treatment guide assessment. Reasoning: authority must follow disclosed criteria. Answer use: evaluation challenges.
- Woods Building Services v Milton Keynes Council: Facts: scoring and manifest error. Held: court scrutinises legality, not merits re-scoring. Reasoning: margin of appreciation in evaluation. Answer use: scoring disputes.
- EnergySolutions EU v Nuclear Decommissioning Authority: Facts: major procurement breach. Held: damages can follow serious breach. Reasoning: causal loss and procurement principles. Answer use: remedies.
- Alstom Transport v Eurostar International: Facts: procurement challenge and suspension. Held: automatic suspension may be lifted under American Cyanamid style balance. Reasoning: public interest and damages adequacy matter. Answer use: interim relief.
- R (Chandler) v Secretary of State for Children, Schools and Families: Facts: public procurement challenge by non-bidder. Held: standing/public law route limited. Reasoning: procurement regime protects economic operators primarily. Answer use: standing.

## Feedback Rules
- Do not treat procurement challenge as ordinary JR without procurement principles.
- Identify limitation/standstill immediately because timing may decide remedy.
- Separate selection criteria from award criteria.
- If current Procurement Act rules are relevant, verify commencement and transitional position.

## Topic-Specific Marking Rubric
- Top-band answers state regime, contracting authority, threshold, stage, principle breached, evidence, limitation, interim relief and remedy.
- Strong essays evaluate transparency, competition, flexibility and value for money with reform context.
- Weaker answers complain about unfair scoring without legal error and remedy timing.

## Strong First-Class Accuracy Pass
- Procurement law is update-sensitive; verify current statutory regime and commencement where relevant.
- Do not treat procurement challenge as ordinary public-law review only.
- Remedies turn on timing: before contract, standstill/automatic suspension; after contract, damages or ineffectiveness where available.

## Full OSCOLA Authority Bank
- Current regime: Procurement Act 2023; Procurement Regulations 2024; National Procurement Policy Statement 2025.
- Legacy/transitional regime: Public Contracts Regulations 2015, SI 2015/102; Utilities Contracts Regulations 2016, SI 2016/274; Concession Contracts Regulations 2016, SI 2016/273.
- Remedies and principles: *Letting International Ltd v London Borough of Newham* [2008] EWHC 1583 (QB); *Woods Building Services v Milton Keynes Council* [2015] EWHC 2011 (TCC); *EnergySolutions EU Ltd v Nuclear Decommissioning Authority* [2017] UKSC 34, [2017] 1 WLR 1373; *Alstom Transport v Eurostar International Ltd* [2010] EWHC 2747 (Ch).

## Current-Law Update Checkpoints
- Procurement Act 2023 came into force on 24 February 2025 for new procurements; legacy procurements and existing contracts may continue under the previous regulations. Always ask when the procurement was commenced/tendered.
- The central digital platform / Find a Tender changes are live under the new regime. Answers should separate regime identification from ordinary transparency/equal-treatment merits.

## Avoid
- Avoid generic fairness complaints without procurement principle and stage.
- Avoid ignoring limitation/standstill timing.
