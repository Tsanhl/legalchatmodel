# Commercial Law

## Answer Method
- Start with the transaction type: sale of goods, agency, retention of title, nemo dat, carriage, secured credit, or payment.
- Problems need title/risk chronology and party-by-party remedies.
- Essays need a thesis on commercial certainty, possession/title tension, documentary sales, or statutory modernisation.
- Keep property, contract, and restitutionary routes distinct.
- Course-style commercial law answers should usually treat business/commercial sales as the centre of gravity. Consumer law is relevant only where the question asks for it or the facts clearly engage consumer status.

## Topic Map
- Sale of Goods Act: implied terms, description, quality, fitness, title, risk, remedies.
- Nemo dat and exceptions; agency authority; retention of title; passing of property.
- Documentary sales, bills of lading, carriage of goods, secured transactions, insolvency priorities.

## Material-Led Emphasis
- Use a fixed chronology: contract formation, passing of property, risk, delivery/acceptance, breach, remedy, and insolvency priority.
- Explain why the rule matters commercially: certainty, speed, documentary reliance, credit risk, insolvency priority, marketability of goods, or allocation of loss.
- Sale-of-goods answers should separate implied terms from rejection/acceptance and damages; do not jump straight to breach.
- Nemo dat answers need the original owner, intermediate transfer, statutory/common-law exception, good faith, possession/title document, and final remedy.
- Retention-of-title answers need clause construction, identifiable goods, mixed/manufactured goods, proceeds, registration/security issue, and insolvency effect.

## For Each Topic Include
- Sale problems: contract status, implied term, breach, acceptance/rejection, damages.
- Title problems: who had title, whether property passed, nemo dat exception, good-faith purchase, remedy.
- Retention of title: clause construction, identifiable goods, proceeds, insolvency effect.

## Case Brief Bank
- Rowland v Divall: Facts: buyer received no title. Held: total failure of consideration. Reasoning: title is fundamental. Answer use: implied title term.
- Arcos v Ronaasen: Facts: goods did not match description exactly. Held: strict description compliance. Reasoning: commercial certainty. Answer use: description.
- Re Moore & Landauer: Facts: packaging discrepancy. Held: breach of description. Reasoning: contractual description matters even if goods usable. Answer use: strict sale terms.
- Romalpa: Facts: retention-of-title clause. Held: seller could retain title to goods. Reasoning: clause can reserve property if drafted effectively. Answer use: ROT.
- Bishopsgate Motor Finance v Transport Brakes: Facts: nemo dat priority dispute. Held: buyer cannot usually get better title than seller. Reasoning: title protection baseline. Answer use: nemo dat.
- Watteau v Fenwick: Facts: undisclosed principal and agent act. Held: principal liable for usual authority in narrow circumstances. Reasoning: agency risk allocation. Answer use: agency authority.

## Feedback Rules
- Do not merge breach of quality with title/risk transfer.
- In insolvency, explain why proprietary title beats unsecured personal claims.
- Retention clauses require precise construction; do not assume proceeds clauses work.

## Strong First-Class Accuracy Pass
- Build a timeline before concluding: contract, identification of goods, passing of property, risk, delivery, acceptance/rejection, onward transfer, insolvency and remedy.
- Nemo dat answers must identify the original owner, each transferor's power, the exact statutory or common-law exception, good faith, possession/title document and the effect on the final buyer.
- Retention-of-title analysis needs clause construction, identifiable goods, mixed or manufactured goods, proceeds, security/registration characterisation and insolvency priority.
- Sale-of-goods answers should separate implied term breach from the buyer's remedy: rejection, acceptance, damages, price claim, termination and restitution have different triggers.
- Agency/commercial authority questions require actual authority, apparent authority, undisclosed principal issues and the third party's knowledge to be handled separately.

## Full OSCOLA Authority Bank
- Sale of goods: Sale of Goods Act 1979, ss 12-15, 16-18, 20, 35, 51 and 53; *Rowland v Divall* [1923] 2 KB 500 (CA); *Arcos Ltd v EA Ronaasen & Son* [1933] AC 470 (HL); *Re Moore & Co Ltd and Landauer & Co* [1921] 2 KB 519 (CA); *Rogers v Parish (Scarborough) Ltd* [1987] QB 933 (CA); *PST Energy 7 Shipping LLC v OW Bunker Malta Ltd* [2016] UKSC 23, [2016] AC 1034.
- Nemo dat and market overt exceptions: Sale of Goods Act 1979, ss 21, 23, 24 and 25; Factors Act 1889, ss 2, 8 and 9; Hire Purchase Act 1964, Pt III; *Bishopsgate Motor Finance Corpn Ltd v Transport Brakes Ltd* [1949] 1 KB 322 (CA); *Cundy v Lindsay* (1878) 3 App Cas 459 (HL); *Phillips v Brooks Ltd* [1919] 2 KB 243 (KB); *Shogun Finance Ltd v Hudson* [2003] UKHL 62, [2004] 1 AC 919; *Newtons of Wembley Ltd v Williams* [1965] 1 QB 560 (CA).
- Retention of title/security: *Aluminium Industrie Vaassen BV v Romalpa Aluminium Ltd* [1976] 1 WLR 676 (CA); *Re Bond Worth Ltd* [1980] Ch 228 (Ch); *Re Peachdart Ltd* [1984] Ch 131 (Ch); *Clough Mill Ltd v Martin* [1985] 1 WLR 111 (CA); *Michael Gerson (Leasing) Ltd v Wilkinson* [2001] QB 514 (CA); *National Westminster Bank plc v Spectrum Plus Ltd* [2005] UKHL 41, [2005] 2 AC 680.
- Agency: *Freeman & Lockyer v Buckhurst Park Properties (Mangal) Ltd* [1964] 2 QB 480 (CA); *Hely-Hutchinson v Brayhead Ltd* [1968] 1 QB 549 (CA); *Watteau v Fenwick* [1893] 1 QB 346 (QB); *Armagas Ltd v Mundogas SA (The Ocean Frost)* [1986] AC 717 (HL).
- Carriage and documents: Carriage of Goods by Sea Act 1992, ss 2-3; *Homburg Houtimport BV v Agrosin Private Ltd (The Starsin)* [2003] UKHL 12, [2004] 1 AC 715.

## Avoid
- Avoid using contract damages when the key issue is proprietary title.
- Avoid title conclusions without timeline.

## Anonymized Quality Upgrade
- A nemo dat essay should organise the exceptions by legal mechanism, use a leading authority for each and group commentary by competing position rather than producing a serial literature list.
- Open with a defensible thesis about the real protection given to innocent purchasers, examine practical reach as well as doctrinal availability, and finish with a reasoned reform position supported by an identified comparator.
