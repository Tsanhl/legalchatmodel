# Corporate Insolvency Law

## Answer Method
- Start with status and timing: solvency, likely insolvency, commencement of administration/liquidation/moratorium, and the relevant decision date.
- Identify the office-holder, asset pool and claimant before discussing directors, transactions, priorities or remedies.
- Problems should separate rescue procedures, creditor enforcement, antecedent transactions, director liability, distributions and cross-border issues.

## For Each Topic Include
- Insolvency trigger and procedure; statutory purpose; moratorium/stay; control of the company.
- Transaction type, look-back period, connected-person rule, defence and remedy.
- Director knowledge/duty at the relevant time, causation/contribution and available relief.
- Security and priority: fixed/floating charge, expenses, preferential claims, prescribed part, unsecured creditors and members.

## Case Brief Bank
- Insolvency Act 1986: principal framework for liquidation, administration, transactions at an undervalue, preferences, wrongful/fraudulent trading and office-holder remedies.
- Corporate Insolvency and Governance Act 2020: inserted the Part A1 moratorium and restructuring plan architecture and strengthened protection of supplies, including Insolvency Act 1986, s 233B, subject to exceptions.
- BTI 2014 LLC v Sequana SA: creditor interests become relevant when directors know or ought to know insolvency is imminent or insolvent liquidation/administration is probable, with content intensifying as financial distress deepens.
- Re Produce Marketing Consortium Ltd (No 2): wrongful-trading analysis focuses on the statutory knowledge point and reasonable steps to minimise creditor loss.

## Strong First-Class Accuracy Pass
- Keep the Companies Act creditor-interest duty, Insolvency Act contribution claims and transaction-avoidance powers analytically separate.
- State who has standing and where any recovery goes; a successful office-holder claim ordinarily benefits the insolvent estate, not one creditor directly.
- Check commencement and temporary/transitional provisions before relying on post-2020 reforms.

## Avoid
- Avoid saying directors must stop trading immediately on any financial difficulty.
- Avoid treating all asset transfers as preferences; undervalue, preference, transaction defrauding creditors and breach of duty have different elements.
