# Remedies

## Answer Method
- Identify the right, wrong, claimant objective and remedial jurisdiction before selecting relief.
- Separate common-law compensation, equitable relief, restitutionary response, proprietary response, public-law order and Human Rights Act remedy.
- Problems should rank a primary remedy, fallback and enforcement/practical obstacles; essays need a thesis about remedial fit, discretion and institutional competence.

## For Each Topic Include
- Cause of action and protected interest; measure/aim of relief; causation/remoteness or discretionary threshold.
- Bars and defences; election/inconsistency; double recovery; third-party effects; enforcement.
- Interim versus final relief; adequacy of damages; clean hands/delay; proportionality and practical order.

## Case Brief Bank
- Senior Courts Act 1981, ss 31 and 37: statutory foundations for judicial-review relief and High Court injunctions, read with the applicable procedural rules and equitable principles.
- Human Rights Act 1998, s 8: a remedy must be just and appropriate; damages are discretionary and Strasbourg principles are relevant.
- American Cyanamid v Ethicon: interim injunction analysis asks serious issue, adequacy of damages and balance of convenience rather than finally trying the merits.
- Cavendish Square Holding BV v Makdessi: agreed-remedy clauses engage the penalty rule where detriment is out of proportion to the innocent party's legitimate interest.
- FHR European Ventures LLP v Cedar Capital Partners LLC: bribes/secret commissions received by a fiduciary are held on constructive trust, enabling proprietary remedies and tracing.

## Strong First-Class Accuracy Pass
- Do not discuss remedies in the abstract: connect each remedy to a proved cause of action and claimant objective.
- Distinguish compensation, gain-based recovery, rescission, restitution after failure, tracing and enforcement against substitutes/recipients.
- Public-law remedies remain discretionary; deal with standing, promptness, alternative remedy, statutory limits and practical effect.

## Avoid
- Avoid assuming an injunction follows because damages are difficult to quantify.
- Avoid double recovery or mixing rescission with affirming damages without addressing election and consistency.
